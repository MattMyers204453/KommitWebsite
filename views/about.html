What is Kommit?

Developing a habit can be difficult. Whether it be going to the gym consistently, studying at the library every morning, or establishing a healthy diet,
many people struggle to commit to a new behaviour and stick to it. This is human nature. The brain is resistant to change. However, with enough repetition, a
previously arduous task becomes effortless.

When most people fail to create a habit, they do so at the beginning. Initially, there is no positive momentum, no neural connections reinforcing the new
activity. The new behaviour feels unnatural and the mind conjures up endless excuses to avoid it. “I’ll do it tomorrow” becomes a daily mantra. So, when a
new gym-goer wakes up early in the morning and is tempted to roll back into bed, he needs an extra push -- at least during this critical period when the
habit is not yet formed. Our app, Kommit, provides this extra push.

Kommit helps people stick with new habits by imposing a form of extreme accountability on its users. The app works in a simple, brutal way. The user types
a place and a time into the app. If the user desires to go to the gym the next morning at 6:30 am, he selects the address of the gym and enters “6:30 am” as
the time. Next, the user deposits an arbitrary amount of money -- say, $25. Both steps are done in less than a minute. When 6:30 am rolls around, Kommit
checks the GPS coordinates of the gym against the coordinates of the user’s phone. If the two locations are not matched up, then the user’s $25 is
automatically donated to charity. Therefore, the user must be at the gym lest he lose $25.

The function of Kommit is straightforward. It simply compares two locations at a given time. This is effective because, although not all habits require being
somewhere, a large subset of them do; and in those cases, being at the right place is half the battle of creating the habit. People decide to skip the gym
while they’re in their warm beds eating chips, not while they are in the gym parking lot with their workout clothes already on. Kommit harnesses a key
psychological fact: the fear of loss is a greater motivator than the opportunity for reward. Users stick to their habits to avoid losing money.
